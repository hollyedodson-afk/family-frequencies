## 0. Confirmed brand facts (from the brief — not assumptions)

- **Family Frequencies is the PARENT brand** — "daytime club culture for families." It is a *container* for many offerings, not a single event. Character: **the frame, not the painting** — confident, grown-up, steady. Tone = **cool party parents**. Explicitly *not* a mum-and-baby class, *not* a council noticeboard, *not* a nightclub.
- **Run by** Holly, Toby & friends. **Based in Mt Maunganui / Tauranga, NZ** (Bay of Plenty).
- **Daylight Disco is the first OFFERING (a sub-brand)** — not the whole brand. Future offerings in the same container: **First Aid Courses, Nutritionist Talks, Family-Friendly Gigs, Workshops.**
- **Events are FREE and WALK-IN. No booking, no ticketing, no accounts.** → Promotion's job is **turnout + email list + following + credibility**, *not* ticket sales. (Ticket-demand mechanics are held in reserve for *future ticketed gigs* only — see Pillar 5.)
- **First Daylight Disco already happened: 4 July 2026, Hide, Mt Maunganui, 12–5pm, free/walk-in.** Vibe: jazz house, kids' boogie songs, face painting, toys. **Today is 6 July 2026 — so we are in genuine afterglow of Event #1, and real event photos/video now exist for the first time.**
- **Audience.** Primary: BOP parents with kids **0–5** who still want a good time and aren't looking for soft-play — they find things on Instagram before a website, trust word of mouth + aesthetic. Secondary: older kids, carers, and friends-without-kids who come along anyway.
- **Distribution reality:** mobile-first, Instagram-led, share-friendly (people arrive from a Story/bio link on their phone).

## 1. Strategy foundation

### The core insight
Parents don't share ads. They share **themselves being good parents at something that looks cool**. Every growth lever runs through one loop:

> **Make a moment worth filming → make attendees the stars → make sharing effortless → new parents see people like them at something that looks like a good party → they turn up (it's free) → they film.**

Because there are **no tickets to sell**, the whole funnel optimises for **turnout + owned audience (email/followers/broadcast)** so that every future offering — the next disco, the first gig, a workshop — launches to a warm, growing crowd. The email list *is* the asset.

### The 5 pillars & why each earns its slot
| Pillar | % | Primary job | Main metric it moves |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Community & UGC** | 40% | Social proof + belonging → turnout | Reach, shares, UGC, repeat attendance |
| **Entertainment** | 25% | Reach to *non-followers* (top of funnel) | Reach, shares, saves |
| **Behind the scenes** | 20% | Trust + bond with the founders + brand recognition | Comments, repeat attendance, brand recall |
| **Education** | 10% | Save-worthy utility (playlists, styling, culture) | Saves, email growth |
| **Direct promotion** | 5% | "It's on, it's free, come" + seed future paid gigs | Turnout, email growth, future ticket demand |

Most "promotion" is **disguised** inside the other four pillars. You almost never post "come to the disco" — you post a toddler losing it on the dancefloor and let the vibe do the quiet work.

### 2026 platform playbook (what's actually working now)
**Instagram**
- **Reels = reach engine**; **carousels win saves + shares** (up to 20 slides, text-forward). Mix both.
- **DM-keyword automation is the #1 list-growth mechanic of 2026:** "Comment DISCO and we'll DM you the details + add you to the list." Drives comments (algorithm loves it) *and* captures the email — perfect for a free event with no checkout to capture people at.
- **Trial Reels:** push new Reels to non-followers first, zero grid risk.
- **Collab posts** (dual-authored) borrow the partner's whole audience — local cafés, kids' brands, the venue (Hide), the DJ, BOP mum creators.
- **Broadcast Channel** = your superfan inner circle: next-date drops, "which venue next?" polls, backstage peeks. Feeds repeat attendance.
- **Hook in first 3s as on-screen text**, not just audio. Watch-time + replays rank you.

**TikTok**
- **Authentic > polished.** Shaky iPhone joy beats produced content.
- **TikTok = search engine.** Put plain-English keywords in caption + on-screen: "family day out Mount Maunganui," "things to do with toddlers Bay of Plenty," "free kids event Tauranga." Free evergreen discovery.
- **Photo mode (carousels)** surging — cheap, high reach.
- **Trending sounds within 3–5 days**; **series/playlists** to bank watch time.

**Facebook**
- Still the **local community + event-discovery powerhouse** for a parent audience.
- **Facebook Groups** (your own "FF Fam" group) + posting into local BOP mum groups = highest-intent reach you have.
- Use **Facebook Events** for every disco (native RSVP → reminder notifications → reach). Perfect for free walk-ins — RSVP ≈ your soft ticket.
- **Cross-post Reels** here; local parents over-index on FB vs IG.

### Cross-platform rule
Shoot **once**, cut for all three. Vertical video is the atomic unit. Captions re-tuned per platform (TikTok = keyword-y/casual, IG = warm + CTA, FB = community + event link).

## 2. Design system — the visual rules EVERY asset must follow

This is the part my first draft got wrong. FF is a **two-tier** system. Get this right and you win *brand recognition* (one of the stated objectives) because the look becomes instantly ownable.

### 2.1 The two tiers: FRAME vs PAINTING
- **FF PARENT = the FRAME.** Used for brand-level content: about/founders, the FF voice, education, cross-offering, the container itself. Palette = **Pacific Dusk**. Confident, grown-up, coastal, daytime. **No dark-primary mode.**
- **OFFERING = the PAINTING.** Each offering has (or will have) its own expressive sub-brand that sits *inside* the FF frame. **Daylight Disco's sub-brand already exists and is LOCKED: illustrated black linework, halftone dots, yellow + blue accents — brighter than the parent palette, on purpose.** It is **never recoloured** to Pacific Dusk. FF appears on it only as a header/stamp + the framed-poster container.

> **Decision rule for any asset:** *Is this about the FF brand, or about a specific event?*
> Brand-level → **Pacific Dusk (frame)**. Event-specific → **event artwork (painting), wrapped in the FF frame**.

### 2.2 Pacific Dusk palette (exact hex)
| Token | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Navy | `#1A2B40` | Primary brand, dark sections, text, borders |
| Navy-deep | `#0E1A2A` | Dark hover |
| Cream | `#FAF0E0` | App background, light text on navy |
| Gold | `#F0B840` | Accent, CTAs, the sun, hard shadow |
| Gold-bright | `#FFC85A` | Gold hover |
| Rose | `#E88070` | Ticker/signup bg, accent pops, **accent words in headlines** |
| Body-on-cream | `#3D4A5C` | Secondary paragraph text |
| Eyebrow gold-brown | `#C99A2E` | Eyebrow labels on cream |

Section rhythm: **cream → navy → cream → rose**, separated by **wave dividers**.

### 2.3 Logo — "daybreak swell"
Gold sun disc above a navy rolling-wave stroke (three arcs = ocean swell + sound frequency + community ripple). Reversed on navy: wave becomes cream, sun stays gold. **Wordmark:** two stacked lines, Barlow Condensed uppercase, `line-height:0.78` — "FAMILY" (weight 600) / "FREQUENCIES" (weight 800, gold on dark / navy on light).

### 2.4 Typography
**Barlow Condensed only** (weights 500–900). Headlines: 800–900, **UPPERCASE**, tight tracking (`-0.015em` to `-0.025em`), line-height `0.8–0.96`. Body: weight 500, ~18–22px, line-height ~1.4, constrained to 30–42ch. **Eyebrow labels:** 13px / 800 / uppercase / `0.18em` tracking. **Accent words inside headlines go rose** (e.g. "*Disco*", "*kids*").

### 2.5 THE signature device — the flat framed poster
This is FF's most ownable visual. Use it as the recurring container for social tiles, carousel covers, event key art:
- **`border: 3px solid #1A2B40`** + **hard offset shadow `8px 8px 0 #F0B840` — NO blur** (flat poster aesthetic). Larger formats use `14px 14px 0`.
- Pills/CTAs `border-radius: 9999px`; cards `20px`.
- **Export note (from the content-plan CLAUDE.md):** when generating downloadable PNGs, **don't use CSS `box-shadow`** — html-to-image rasterises it unreliably; draw the hard shadow as a real offset `<div>` layer. And **embed the Barlow Condensed font** (`ensureFonts()` + double-render) or text reflows on export.

### 2.6 Voice
Cool, warm, a little cheeky, aesthetic-led. Party-parent energy, never twee, never corporate, never council-noticeboard. Short lines. Kiwi. Confident that a good time doesn't stop when you have kids.

### 2.7 Reusable Claude Design prompt headers
Prepend the right header to every design prompt so assets stay on-system:

**[FRAME] — brand-level assets**
> *"Design for Family Frequencies (parent brand, 'the frame not the painting'). Palette Pacific Dusk: navy #1A2B40, cream #FAF0E0, gold #F0B840, rose #E88070. Typeface Barlow Condensed, uppercase headlines weight 800–900, tight tracking, accent words in rose. Include the daybreak-swell logo (gold sun disc over a navy three-arc wave). Use the signature flat framed-poster treatment: 3px navy border + hard 8px 8px gold offset shadow, no blur. Coastal, daytime, confident, grown-up. No dark-primary; cream or navy grounds only. Then: …"*

**[PAINTING] — Daylight Disco (event) assets**
> *"Design for the Daylight Disco offering. Its locked sub-brand is illustrated black linework with halftone dots and yellow + blue accents — brighter and more expressive than the parent palette, and it is NEVER recoloured to Pacific Dusk. Present it INSIDE the Family Frequencies frame: wrap the event artwork in the signature flat framed poster (3px navy #1A2B40 border + hard 8px 8px gold #F0B840 shadow, no blur), with a small FF daybreak-swell stamp/header. Barlow Condensed for any FF-frame text. Then: …"*

## 3. The weekly content engine (repeatable "franchises")

Run **named recurring slots**. Audiences love appointment content; your team loves not deciding from scratch; it's trivially automatable. Cadence for a busy team: **5 feed posts/week + daily-ish Stories + 1 Broadcast drop/week**, ramping Stories in the 10 days before an event.

| Day | Franchise | Pillar | Format | Recurring hook pattern |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Mon** | *Monday Mood* | Entertainment | Reel | Relatable parent POV → "come dance it out" |
| **Tue** | *Two-Left-Feet Tuesday* | Education | Carousel/Reel | Playlist, styling, culture, dance move of the week |
| **Wed** | *Behind the frequencies* | BTS | Reel/Story | Founder voice, event build, "what goes into it" |
| **Thu** | *Throwback / UGC feature* | Community & UGC | Reel/Carousel | Repost attendee footage: "THIS is a Daylight Disco" |
| **Fri** | *Feel-Good Friday* | Community/Entertainment | Reel | Best joyful clip of the week; high-share |
| **Sat/Sun** | *Live event OR weekend Story-takeover* | Community & UGC | Stories + 1 Reel | Real-time capture or curated weekend joy |
| **Weekly** | *FF Fam Broadcast drop* | BTS/Promo | Broadcast | Insider news, next-date, polls |

Over a 4-week cycle this self-balances to **~40/25/20/10/5**. **Event weeks override the grid** (see §5).

## 6B. Automated content CREATION — but keep it real

You also asked about **content-creation** platforms (making the posts, not just scheduling them). Here's the honest strategist's take: **most "AI content creation platforms" are wrong for FF**, and using them would actively hurt you.

### The trap to avoid
The big open-source names — **MoneyPrinterTurbo** (~90k★, MIT), **ShortGPT**, **OpenShorts** — generate *faceless* videos: AI script → AI voiceover → generic stock footage / AI avatars. They're built for anonymous affiliate/YouTube-shorts channels. **FF's entire 2026 advantage is the opposite:** real footage of real families having a real good time. AI-stock "family joy" reads as fake, and "authentic > polished" is the #1 ranking + trust signal on TikTok/IG right now. **Don't generate synthetic FF content.** Your moat is that yours is real.

### What "automated creation" *should* mean for FF
Automate the **boring production around real footage**, not the footage itself. Four layers, in ROI order — and you already own the best one:

| Layer | Job | Best fit for FF | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Programmatic branded graphics/video** | Turn a data row into an on-brand carousel/cover/lower-third/animated tile | **Remotion + Hyperframes (you already have these skills installed)** | *The killer fit.* Renders YOUR design system — framed poster, Pacific Dusk, Barlow Condensed — from the content sheet. On-brand by construction, not AI-slop. Feed it the §4 recipe fields → out come C2 photo-frames, ED1 song-cards, P1 date cards, subtitle lower-thirds. |
| **2. Repurpose real footage → shorts** | Cut 5 hrs of disco footage into 15–20 vertical clips | **[AI-Youtube-Shorts-Generator](https://github.com/samuraigpt/ai-youtube-shorts-generator)** (OSS Opus Clip alt) or **Opus Clip** (SaaS) | LLM highlight detection + auto 9:16 crop + captions. Turns one shoot into a month of C1/E2/Feel-Good clips. This is the automation with the biggest time payoff. |
| **3. Auto-captions/subtitles** | Burn-in subs (non-negotiable for Reels) | **Whisper** (open source) in your n8n pipeline | Free, accurate, runs headless. |
| **4. AI copy + still-image design** | Draft captions, generate supporting graphics | **Claude** (you have it) for copy in the FF voice §2.6; **Postiz built-in AI/designer** or **Canva API** for images | Keep a human on final approval. |

### The FF content factory (assembled from what you already have)
```

      ├─▶ [Layer 2] auto-clip → 15–20 vertical shorts        ← AI-Shorts-Generator / Opus Clip
      │        │
      │        └─▶ [Layer 3] Whisper burn-in captions        ← open source, in n8n
```

Everything mechanical is automated; the one irreplaceable input — real families, real joy — stays human. **Net effect:** one monthly shoot + one batch render = a month of on-brand, authentic content, mostly hands-off.

4. **UGC harvester (highest ROI)** — hashtag/mention trigger → auto-DM thanks + permission → save asset + create a Thursday-slot draft. The UGC slot fills itself.

**Stay human on purpose:** first-hour comment/DM replies, final caption approval, reading the room on trends. Everything mechanical → automated.

## 7B. How the running system works, step by step (operational walkthrough)

Once live, the whole thing is **one flywheel + four pipelines**. Your hands-on job shrinks to three things: shoot once a month, tap approve, be human in the comments for the first hour.

```
        YOU (once a month)                    AUTOMATED (every week, hands-off)
   ┌─────────────────────────┐        ┌──────────────────────────────────────────┐
   │ 1. Film one event/shoot │───────▶│ A. Content factory: clip + caption + brand │
   │ 2. Approve on Telegram  │        │ B. Scheduler (yours): queue → post IG/FB/TT │
   │ 3. Reply to comments 1hr│◀───────│ C. Growth loop: comment → DM → email list  │
   └─────────────────────────┘        │ D. UGC harvester: fan footage → your feed  │
                                      └──────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Monthly rhythm (your ~half-day of real work)** — ideally right after each disco while footage is fresh:
1. Film the event + a short founder-franchise batch (GRWM/real-talk/origin, playlist voiceover). One session.
2. Dump everything into the Cloudinary/Drive folder.
3. Confirm the pre-filled 4-week grid in the queue (dates + asset links). That's your input.

**Pipeline A — content factory (after batch day):**
1. Auto-clipper cuts hours of footage into 15–20 vertical 9:16 clips (highlight detection).
2. Whisper auto-burns captions onto each clip.
3. Remotion/Hyperframes renders on-brand graphics from each sheet row (framed-poster covers, song-cards, date cards, lower-thirds) in Pacific Dusk or DD-artwork-in-frame.
4. Claude drafts each caption in the FF voice from the recipe hook + pillar.
5. Assets + captions land in the queue, `status: ready`.

**Pipeline B — scheduler (your existing system, daily):**
1. Reads the queue, finds today's posts.
2. Telegram: preview + caption + APPROVE/RESCHEDULE.
3. You tap APPROVE (~10s).
4. Posts to IG/FB (+TikTok) at the optimal window; auto-creates the FB Event on announcements.

**Pipeline C — growth loop (24/7):** comment `DISCO`/`PLAYLIST` → auto-DM details/link **+ add to Kit list with a tag**. Every post becomes an email-capture machine.

**Pipeline D — UGC harvester (24/7):** monitors #FFDanceOff/#DaylightDisco/@mentions → auto-DMs thanks + permission → on "yes," saves the clip + drops a draft into Thursday's slot. The UGC feed fills itself.

**Event week override:** P1/countdown pre-scheduled from batch day → "it's this weekend, free" reminder fires Thu/Fri → you shoot live Stories day-of → within 48h the recap batch (C1/C2/P3) auto-generates and the next afterglow begins.

**What you actually touch, weekly:**
| Task | Who | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Film event + founder clips | You | ~½ day/month |
| Approve posts | You (Telegram tap) | ~1 min/day |
| First-hour comment/DM replies | You | ~15 min/post |
| Everything else | Automated | 0 |

Plus a Monday Telegram **digest** (reach, saves, shares, top post, follower + email growth) — a 2-minute read that steers next cycle.

> **Build status:** Pipelines B (scheduler) and much of C/D exist in your n8n already. **Pipeline A (the content factory) is the net-new piece** — that's what we're building next.
