# Family Frequencies — Brand Identity Design

**Date:** 2026-06-20
**Status:** Approved

---

## Overview

Family Frequencies is a family-friendly events and community project based in Mt Maunganui / Tauranga, NZ. The brand needs to work as a **masterbrand** — a flexible parent identity that holds multiple event sub-brands (Daylight Disco, first aid courses, nutritionist talks, future gigs, workshops) without competing with them.

The visual direction is **summer festival, daytime, coastal** — warm and energetic but not nightclub dark, not soft or childish.

---

## Brand Architecture

**Parent brand:** Family Frequencies — present on all materials but deliberately restrained. The frame, not the painting.

**Event sub-brands:** Each event has its own expressive identity that sits under the parent brand. The Daylight Disco illustrated artwork (black linework, halftone dots, yellow/blue accents, parents + babies DJing) is **locked in** — it is the first sub-brand and sets the tone for how events can be loud and specific under a quieter parent.

**Relationship:** Sub-brands bring colour, illustration, and energy. The parent brand provides the system, the stamp, and the recognisable container.

---

## Symbol

A set of **three concentric wave arcs** — organic and slightly irregular in character, reading simultaneously as:
- Ocean swell / coastal waves (geographic, Mt Maunganui)
- Sound/frequency rings (earns the "Frequencies" name)
- Ripple rings from a stone dropped in water (community, togetherness)

**What it is not:** the digital zigzag waveform (too tech/music-industry generic). The arcs should feel closer to a cross-section of the ocean horizon — natural, not signal-processed.

**Scale requirement:** Must read clearly at stamp size (~20px height on print) and hold as an Instagram avatar (circular crop). Three arcs is the maximum — any more collapses at small sizes.

---

## Wordmark

**"FAMILY FREQUENCIES"** — all caps, stacked as two lines.

- **Type style:** Bold condensed sans-serif. The name is long; condensed keeps it compact across all surfaces.
- **Weight contrast:** "FAMILY" slightly lighter weight than "FREQUENCIES" — creates a title/descriptor hierarchy without needing different type sizes.
- **Character:** Confident and grown-up. Not playful or hand-drawn. The events are playful — the parent brand is the steady hand.

---

## Colour Palette

**Locked direction: Pacific Dusk** — chosen from visual exploration (v1–v3 comparison). Deep navy base with warm gold and blush rose accents.

| Role | Hex | Description | Usage |
|------|-----|-------------|-------|
| **Navy** | `#1A2B40` | Deep coastal navy — the primary background | Website header, IG header strip, poster stamp on dark contexts |
| **Cream** | `#FAF0E0` | Warm off-white | Type on navy, light surface backgrounds |
| **Gold** | `#F0B840` | Warm gold — the primary accent | Wave symbol on navy, "FREQUENCIES" wordmark accent, highlight text |
| **Rose** | `#E88070` | Blush rose — secondary accent | Secondary detail, date/location text, gradient partner to Gold |

**What sub-brands bring:** Full saturation, illustration, photography, event-specific palettes. Daylight Disco's yellow and blue are brighter and more expressive than the parent palette — that contrast is intentional and correct.

**Poster stamp collision:** Navy stamp on the Daylight Disco poster (which is light/yellow/blue) reads as a proper frame rather than disappearing into the background. Gold wave arcs on navy are legible at stamp size.

---

## Logo Configurations

Four lockups required, in order of priority:

1. **Horizontal lockup** — symbol left, wordmark right. For: website header, email, letterhead.
2. **Stacked lockup** — symbol above wordmark. For: Instagram post headers, signage, square crops.
3. **Symbol only** — wave arcs without wordmark. For: Instagram avatar, favicon, app icon, watermark.
4. **Event stamp** — small horizontal lockup with "A FAMILY FREQUENCIES EVENT" replacing the wordmark. For: bottom of event posters (Daylight Disco and all future events).

---

## Surface Behaviour

### Website
- Horizontal lockup in the header (ink on sand, or reversed on a dark section)
- Event listing cards use each event's own sub-brand colour/artwork
- Footer: symbol only, or stacked lockup small

### Instagram
- **Avatar:** Symbol only, ocean on sand (or ink on sand)
- **Event announcement posts:** Consistent header strip — stacked lockup in ink/sand — followed by the event's own full-colour artwork and details. The header strip is the "frame"; the event content fills the rest.
- **Stories:** Event-led; parent brand appears as a small stamp

### Print / Event Posters
- "A FAMILY FREQUENCIES EVENT" stamp, bottom-right or bottom-centre
- Ink on white, or reversed (sand on ink) depending on the poster's background
- The stamp should not exceed ~10% of the poster's shorter dimension

---

## What This Is Not

- Not a nightclub brand (no dark backgrounds as the primary mode)
- Not soft or pastel (this is "cool party parents", not playgroup)
- Not icon-heavy (the wave mark is the one symbol — no additional music/sound icons at brand level)
- Not photography-dependent (the parent brand system works without photos; events use photography or illustration as they choose)

---

## First Execution: Daylight Disco (July 4, 2026)

The first event page and poster uses the existing Daylight Disco illustrated artwork. The Family Frequencies parent brand appears as:
- The event stamp on the poster
- The website header above the event content
- The Instagram header strip before the Daylight Disco artwork

The Daylight Disco artwork does **not** need to adopt the Family Frequencies palette — it keeps its yellow, blue, and black linework. The stamp is the only parent brand element on the poster.

## Out of Scope
