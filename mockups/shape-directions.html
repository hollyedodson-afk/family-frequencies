Family Frequencies: Three Brand Directions

A shape board for choosing the parent brand lane before making the website. These are strategic directions, not the old A/B/C mockups.

Decision to make

Which world should Family Frequencies own: sunlit parent club, neighbourhood broadcast, or family gig crew?

Direction 1

Daytime Club Culture

Cool, local, social, music-led, and parent-practical.

Core ideaA daytime club for people who still love music, now with small humans in tow.
Best forMaking the brand feel aspirational, social, grown-up, and useful.
RiskNeeds real/social imagery or strong art direction, otherwise it can fall flat.
Palette
Sample language

Good music before bedtime.

Daytime events for little dancers and grown-up people. Free entry, useful details, proper tunes.

Daylight Disco: the launch party, loud and illustrated.

First aid/nutrition: practical sessions framed as part of the same local parent culture.

My vote: strongest parent brand, most human, least gimmicky.
Direction 2

Community Radio / Frequencies

A softer broadcast system for events, talks, gigs, and recurring family programming.

Core ideaFamily Frequencies as a local station: tune into what is on for families.
Best forFlexibility across music, education, wellness, workshops, and community sessions.
RiskCan become too conceptual or editorial if it loses the party energy.
Palette
Sample language

Tune into the next family frequency.

Music, useful chats, workshops, and local happenings for families who want something with a bit of pulse.

Daylight Disco: the first broadcast, bigger colour and louder sound.

First aid/nutrition: easy to programme as episodes, sessions, or guest slots.

Best if the brand will become a broad community calendar.
Direction 3

Family-Friendly Gig Crew

Louder, more promoter-led, and closest to an event crew with proper posters.

Core ideaA crew putting on excellent daytime gigs and useful events for families.
Best forMomentum, posters, lineups, social sharing, and event-by-event excitement.
RiskCan make practical workshops feel like a stretch unless the system has quieter modes.
Palette
Sample language

Family gigs, daytime volume.

Events with a lineup feel: music first, family-friendly by design, practical enough for real life.

Daylight Disco: very natural fit, this lane almost wants posters immediately.

First aid/nutrition: possible, but would need a calmer sub-format.

Best for hype and shareability, weaker for the broader parent-support side.

Recommended blend

Use Daytime Club Culture as the parent brand. Borrow Community Radio for programming structure, then use Gig Crew for event posters and launch energy. That gives Family Frequencies a real human world, not just music symbols.
